# Impeccable Design Context — Capoeira Web Admin

## Design Context

### Users
Single super-admin (the product owner, Javier). Uses the panel on desktop during focused management sessions — reviewing data, resolving requests, monitoring finances, sending notifications. The mindset is: efficient and in control. He needs to see everything quickly and act without friction. Not a casual user; someone who knows the platform deeply and wants speed over hand-holding.

### Brand Personality
**Three words: disciplined, alive, grounded.**

Capoeira is a Brazilian martial art that is simultaneously a dance, a fight, and a game. The brand should feel athletic and purposeful — not corporate SaaS, not generic startup. There is energy here, rhythm, and precision. The admin panel is the backstage of that world: it should feel like a command center built for someone who takes their craft seriously.

Anti-references: generic blue SaaS admin, Material Design dashboards, Bootstrap admin templates, anything that looks like it could be for any product.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Theme**: Dark mode. Admin sessions are focused work, often at night or in concentrated bursts. Dark reduces eye strain and puts data front and center.
- **Tone**: Utilitarian precision with character. Think: a professional sound mixing board, a sports analytics dashboard, a well-worn notebook with careful annotations.
- **Color**: Deep slate base (`oklch(0.13 0.015 250)`). A strong blue primary (`oklch(0.55 0.18 250)`) for actions. Warm amber (`oklch(0.72 0.15 75)`) as secondary accent — nodding to the earthy tones of capoeira (sand, wood, sun). Muted greens for positive states.
- **Typography**: Needs two distinct voices — a technical/data voice (numbers, tables, labels) and a display voice (section headers, empty states). Avoid monospace-as-"technical" cliché. The display font should feel handcrafted, with personality. The data font should be clean and legible at small sizes.
- **No gradient text. No side-stripe borders on cards. No glowing cyan accents.**

### Design Principles
1. **Command, don't browse.** Every page should let the admin act immediately — filters, bulk actions, quick edits within the table row. Minimize navigation for common tasks.
2. **Data first, chrome second.** Tables and metrics take priority. The UI frame (sidebar, topbar) should recede. The content is the star.
3. **States are first-class.** Empty states, loading states, and error states should be designed with the same care as the primary state. They are not afterthoughts.
4. **Rhythm over uniformity.** Not every section looks the same. The financial dashboard has different visual weight than the requests queue. Let content dictate layout, not templates.
5. **Trust through precision.** Numbers are formatted. Dates are consistent. Statuses are unambiguous. When in doubt, show more information, not less.
